Music Compositions for Double Bass, Piano, and String Orchestra by Murray "Doc" Solomon

Murray "Doc" Solomon was born in The Big Apple, New York City, USA. He is a successful veteran double bassist, music educator and composer for the double bass, piano and string orchestra. In his childhood he played piano in Fox Movietone films and began to play the double bass upon entering Music & Art High School, studying with Fred Zimmerman of the New York Philharmonic Orchestra, one of the most important American pedagogues and transcribers of music for the double bass. In his teens “Doc” had his own radio show, The Bobby Sox Canteen. He received a Bachelor of Science degree from New York University and a Masters degree from Columbia University, majoring in Music Education.

Subsequently, he worked in every style and combination of bass playing for jazz, big band, radio, television, jingles and symphony along with 26 years with the Broadway Music Theatre Orchestras. He toured the Far East on a Presidential Cultural Exchange Concert Tour. Alongside his performing career “Doc” worked as a Music Education String Specialist for 28 years, and has arranged much music publications including “John Denver Meets the String Orchestra”

Murray "Doc" Solomon studied composition with Wallingford Reigger, an American prize winner, and Josef Schmid, a member of the Schoenberg Group. His works for the double bass, piano and string orchestra are known for their character, spirit, life and rhythmic vibrancy and have been performed throughout the world.

A tribute to Jobim, King of the Bossa Nova. A pizzi melody develops into a sequential arco tune, in which each bassist plays some bossa rhythm, counter melody, harmony & melody.

MS#107
Descant Blues

Premiered as part of a Doc Solomon Celebration Concert at Bass Fest 2002 in England. The use of a musical democracy allows each bassist to play the contents of all the other parts after being introduced separately and ending with a Count Bassie tag. Blues fun for all.
